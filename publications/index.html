Hypothesis Testing for the Scale Parameter of Exponential Family of Distributions

Parsa H. Rafsanjani, Adel Mohammadpour

This article presents a unified framework for testing hypotheses regarding the scale parameter of exponential family of distributions. Uniformly most powerful (UMP) tests are constructed for simple and one-sided hypotheses, and it is proved that no UMP test exists for the two-tailed case. Instead, a uniformly most powerful unbiased (UMPU) test is derived for that case. A discussion of Generalized Likelihood Ratio tests is also included. The algorithms for these tests are implemented in R and applied to compute and compare results across a wide range of examples from various well-known distributions.
