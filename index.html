Open-Source and GNU/Linux

Beyond academia, I'm a GNU/Linux enthusiast. I began using GNU/Linux in 2020, starting with Ubuntu before exploring other distributions like Fedora, Linux Mint, and Pop!_OS. Having used Pop!_OS as my primary OS for several years, I transitioned to Arch Linux and have experienced no major issues so far.

My main motivation was to deepen my understanding of Linux. Arch has taught me what questions to ask when configuring my system for my workflow, and how little is actually needed to perform my computations. Unlike versioned distributions, Arch follows a rolling-release model (thereâ€™s just one Arch Linux, not Arch 1.0, Arch 2.0, etc.), which I see as one of its greatest strengths.
